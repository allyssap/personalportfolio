Compound Interest Visualizer

About

This was a project was brought to me by a math teacher at St. Davids highschool in Waterloo. As part of the new Grade 9 curriculum, students need to be introduced to compound interest. Mrs. Keller came to me with the problem: students need to understand compound interest, but they do not need to learn to calculate it.

The site is created with HTML and CSS. I used JavaScript to allow the students to input their numbers with sliders, and see the effects of changing the down payment amount, interst rate and time will affect the loan. I implemented Google Charts to show the changes in loan amounts.

Goals and Accomplishments

With this project, my main goal was to provide Mrs. Keller with a working product, that she and other Grade 9 teachers can use to demonstrate how compound interest looks on a $1000 loan.

This was my first experence coding with JavaScript to make my website more interactive and to display the inputted data into a chart. I found it fun to create the function and was very motivated by the fact that my project would be used by students to improve their learning. My next goal is to further my learning in JavaScript by learning React or Vue.js.
